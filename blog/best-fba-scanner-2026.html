The Real Cost Nobody Talks About

Every FBA scanner comparison you'll find online lists the monthly subscription price. What they don't mention is that almost every scanner requires a separate Keepa API subscription to access sales rank history, price trends, and seller count data.

Keepa costs a minimum of $21/month on top of your scanner subscription. So that "$28/month" scanner? It's really $49/month. That "$69/month" scanner? $90/month.

With one exception — but we'll get to that.

The Contenders

SellerAmp (SAS) — $27.95/month + Keepa

The most popular scanner among FBA sellers. SellerAmp has solid data and a comprehensive feature set, but it's a web-based app that runs in a browser wrapper on mobile. This means slower performance, occasional crashes (check the App Store reviews), and that distinctly non-native feel. Real cost with Keepa: $48.95+/month.

Scoutify 2 (InventoryLab) — $69/month + Keepa

Bundled with InventoryLab's accounting platform, Scoutify is feature-rich but expensive. It's aimed at full-time sellers who need listing and accounting tools alongside scanning. If you just need a scanner, you're paying for a lot of features you won't use. Real cost with Keepa: $90+/month.

ScoutIQ — $44/month + Keepa

Originally built for book sellers, ScoutIQ has expanded to general products. The database approach (local data vs. live API calls) makes it fast but sometimes outdated. Missing Amazon seller detection. Real cost with Keepa: $65+/month.

Profit Bandit — $9.99/month + Keepa

The budget option. Profit Bandit gets the basics right at a low price point, but it lacks advanced features like competition trends, price history visualization, and Amazon seller warnings. You get what you pay for. Real cost with Keepa: $30.99+/month.

What Actually Matters in a Scanner

After hundreds of sourcing trips and thousands of scans, here's what separates a good scanner from a great one:

  1. Speed. If it takes more than 2 seconds to show results, you're losing deals to the person next to you with a faster app.
  2. Break-even cost. You should know the maximum you can pay before you look at the price tag. This one number saves more time than any other feature.
  3. Amazon seller detection. If Amazon owns the buy box, walk away. Period. Your scanner should scream this at you.
  4. Real data, not estimates. Sales rank history, not guesses. Actual seller counts, not "low/medium/high." Price trends over 90 days, not just today's snapshot.
  5. No hidden costs. The subscription price should be the price. No surprise Keepa fees, no "premium data" upsells.
